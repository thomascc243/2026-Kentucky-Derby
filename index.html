The Formula

How the value score works

This formula finds horses the public is systematically under-betting in exotic pools relative to their actual ability. Three inputs drive every score.

Value Score = (Implied Probability × 0.35) + (Pool Scarcity × 0.40) + (Payout Multiplier × 0.25)
Implied Probability — converted from morning-line odds (1 ÷ (odds+1) × 100)
Pool Scarcity — how rarely the horse appears in public exacta/trifecta pools (100 − pool %)
Payout Multiplier — scaled odds-based return potential
Key insight — scarcity gets the highest weight because market inefficiency lives there

The formula doesn't predict winners. It identifies horses whose true probability of hitting the board is higher than what public pricing implies. In a race with a contested pace, that gap widens for closers and stalkers.

Race Context

Pace scenario — why it matters today

Three confirmed front-runners are expected to fight for the lead: Ezum (led wire-to-wire at Colonial Downs), Exhibition Only (inside post, likely to send early), and Corona de Oro (set pace at Fair Grounds). When speed horses battle each other, closers and stalkers win at a disproportionately high rate.

The pace trap: The public will bet Ezum heavily because his 19.5-length maiden win looks explosive. But that win came on a soft Colonial Downs track with no pace pressure. Today he gets contested fractions for the first time. The formula lowers his composite score because his pool saturation is extremely high relative to the genuine uncertainty about his stamina and pace tolerance.

Confessional is the horse who benefits most. He's a proven stalker, already won at Keeneland, carries the field's highest Brisnet speed figure (95), and has Irad Ortiz Jr. aboard — the same jockey who won the Blue Grass last week.

Honest Limitations

What the formula doesn't know

Ezum's Colonial Downs win: A 19.5-length blowout can mean a genuinely exceptional horse OR a track bias on a fast surface with weak competition. The formula can't distinguish. The Trifecta B ticket covers you if he fires anyway.

The Hell We Did at distance: His scarcity score is high and his Sunland Park sprint was eye-catching (13 lengths, 93 Brisnet). But he has never raced beyond 6 furlongs. Stretching to 1 1/16 miles is a genuine unknown.

Pool % are estimates: Scarcity scores are based on typical public betting behavior at these morning-line odds. Real tote pool percentages update live on your app 30–60 minutes before post. If Confessional's exacta pool % drops under 10%, that's a strong buy signal to increase your exacta stake.

The formula's edge is process, not magic. It will be wrong some years. The goal is to have the right horses on the ticket at the right price consistently — so the scarcity-driven hits more than cover the misses over time.
